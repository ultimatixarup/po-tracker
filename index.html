About Kat

A consultant who's
actually run the place.

Not theory. Kat has built ranches from raw acreage and managed them day to day — she knows where plans break in practice.

"Most ranch projects don't fail on vision. They fail on sequencing, on who actually shows up, and on details nobody budgeted for."

Kat has spent her career on both sides of the fence line: as a horsewoman managing daily operations, and as the person who plans and stands up a ranch from the ground floor — site layout, fencing and footing, barns and arenas, water and pasture rotation, and the herd management systems that keep it all running once the dust settles.

She works directly with owners as a project lead, translating a vision into a buildable plan and then staying on through execution — coordinating the contractors, vets, farriers, and feed suppliers who actually get the work done.

How an engagement runs

From bare land
to running ranch.

Every project moves through the same four stages — Kat stays on through all of them.

01

Assess & Plan

Walk the land, evaluate existing structures, and build a phased plan for layout, fencing, footing, water, and herd capacity.
02

Build the Network

Bring in the right contractors, farriers, vets, and feed and equipment suppliers from Kat's vetted network — matched to the project's scale and budget.
03

Manage Construction

Run point on the build: scheduling, site visits, and quality checks, so owners get one accountable contact instead of a dozen subcontractors.
04

Operate & Hand Off

Stand up daily operating systems — feeding, turnout, herd health routines — and train staff until the ranch runs without her in the room.
